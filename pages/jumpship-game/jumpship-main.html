For reasons unknown to even the Terran Mandate, the sector known as Cornu Curta is abnormally difficult for spike drive equipped ships to navigate. While spike ships operate normally in the outer edge of a star system's gravity well (the 80 to 100 distance bands), operation deeper within a star system's gravity well or too close to a planet, or object with a similar gravitic effect, is impossible for spike drive ships, requiring tows or tugs in order to traverse the interior of a star system.

For this reason, spike drive equipped space craft, referred to locally as jumpships, normally mount a large quantity of shiptender mounts with which to ferry system ships through space and stick to the edge of a star system, only going as deep as they need to reach refuel and restocking stations.

Another grand mystery of Cornu Curta is the fact that human level intellect VIs occasionally show arcane powers, though they are incapable of accessing psionics, while human level intellect AIs are the opposite, with rare individuals able to tap into psionic abilities, but unable to grasp the fundamental interactions required for magic.

Potential Starts:

  • Mayrifa - A warm world with inedible, toxic local flora where the prior masters of the sector revolt against the relative new comers who have taken their power away, necessitating a reluctant occupation by the Dechtum Ardurix.
  • Antino - A cold-to-temperate world with only the remnants of the once verdant ecosystem. After the nearly exclusively male led government and businesses over-exploited the environment, the female population rose up and took control. Now men find themselves second class citizens and the entire population must have either cybernetics or genetic modification to be able to survive long term exposure to the industrial chemicals that still taint the soil and atmosphere. Exposure of less than 3 months with at least a week to purge afterward is normally enough, with doses under 1 year treatable by TL 4 medicine within 2 months.
  • Ingigud - A temperate planet with a thick atmosphere (pressure mask required) and a human-miscible biosphere. The local population is waging a vicious "holy war," though due to the low population of the planet and non-breathable atmosphere (without a basic respirator, at least), the war is being fought almost exclusively with robots and has only claimed 12 lives so far, after a misfire from attacking robots hit a residence's door seals and leaked enough atmosphere into the home to kill the occupants. All buildings are now required to have a "respirator alarm."
  • Dynomed - A temperate-to-warm planet with a human-miscible biosphere, Dynomed is teeming with refugees from the Thyra system, who the locals blame for the recent zombie outbreak that has forced them back to TL3 technology.
  • Melis - A temperate and breathable planet with a native biosphere nearing the end of the microbial stage of evolution, the technology confiscated from refugees to the planet has allowed rapid advancement and a steady growth of power an influence. Melis is considered to be on track to become the ruling power in the Queel League, as the deep space stations that provide the Queel League with its limited advanced technology struggle to keep up with the industrial output of a planet.
  • Helga - A cold-to-temperate planet a thick atmosphere and no native biosphere, the local military lords sacrifice citizens, primarily criminals, to maintain their extended lifespan as they struggle to hunt down rogue super soldier programs and the cults that fund them.
