Appearance and Biology

The ijudi are feathered amphibious humanoids that typically stand around 2.2 meters (7' 3") tall. The ijudi have slimy, feathered skin that is capable of changing colors from dull browns and greens to bright blues, yellows and purples, large, frog-like heads with poison glands on the sides of their neck and large, strong arms that unfold into either small fins or large wings, while ijudi feet are heavily webbed. Ijudi flight is clumsy and well optimized for gliding, long distance travel. In the water, however, ijudi are fast, nimble predators that use a mix of ambush and rush-down hunting strategies.

The ijudi's natural diet consists of roughly any animal cat-sized or smaller that an ijudi can fit into their mouth in a single bite. Ijudi are immune to any poison in the food they eat as their bodies sequester it in their poison glands. This immunity does not protect them from poisons that are absorbed through a means other than their digestive tract.

The ijudi posses almost no sexual dimorphism.

Government, Structure, and Society

The ijudi organize themselves into tribes that control roughly state sized tracts of land that are lead by a chieftain, who then has family elders that rule smaller tracts of land below them and keep the chieftain appraised of the goings on in their territory. Acquiring chieftainhood is normally about convincing your tribe that you have enough support to challenge the chieftain and then convincing said chieftain to stepdown peacefully, either through false influence or through deniable actions.

Planetary decisions are made at monthly moots where all the chieftains meet and decide on what policies they will all follow, typically to ensure that they have protections against foreign powers and to ensure that internal threats don't spread between tribes. While the moots technically have the authority to enforce policies that are focused inward, the personal interests of individual chieftains and tribes make it so these types of policies almost never make it through a moot. Federal/imperial level decisions are made at a twice annual moot of the chieftains decided by the moots to represent their planet. Similar to planetary moots, imperial moots rarely make much headway on policies other than collective defense.

Technology

Due to the ijudi's ability to traverse land, air, and fresh water naturally, their technology is designed to operate in any environment, while their traditionalism leads them to prefer proven technologies over unproven, but innovative ones.

  • All ijudi technology is waterproof and capable of being submerged up to half a kilometer of depth
  • All ijudi technology reduces the ranges to damage the gear by 1 (AWN 73)
  • Ballistic Weapons
    • Ijudi made ballistic weapons operate normally underwater and in space
    • Ijudi made ballistic weapons have active recoil mitigation that grants a +1 to attack rolls
  • Mag Weapons
    • Ijudi made mag weapons deal +2 damage
  • Explosive Weapons
    • Ijudi made explosive weapons increase their damage dice by 1
    • Ijudi made explosive weapons increase their encumbrance by 1, unless they have a magazine value (such as the rocket launcher) in which case their ammunition increases its encumbrance by 1 per unit
    • Ijudi made explosive weapons increase their damage against underwater targets by an extra damage dice
  • Laser Weapons
    • Ijudi made laser weapons fire through water and the water-air interface normally
    • Ijudi made laser weapons produce no sound or visible light when fired
    • Ijudi made laser weapons only take a -2 penalty to hit when firing through thermally-resistant particulate matter
  • Thermal/Plasma Weapons
    • Ijudi do not make plasma weapons
  • Armor
    • Ijudi made armor with the Heavy property does not apply a penalty to stealth
