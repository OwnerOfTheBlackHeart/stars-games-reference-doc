Fellowship of Steel

The Fellowship of Steel is a group of technophiles who believe that working with advanced technology is the fastest way to enlightenment, viewing hording and utilizing such technologies as an almost religious duty. The Fellowship's reverence towards advanced technology and paramilitary nature lead to the Fellowship's heavy use of mechs and protomechs.

Technology

The Fellowship of Steel focuses on mixing heavily armored and armored, fast strike infantry with mechs and high speed, airborne vehicles.

  • Mechs
    • Fellowship of Steel produced mechs have the following benefits based off of size:
      • Light - +5 mass and power, +2 armor
      • Medium - +10 mass and power, +3 armor
      • Heavy - +15 mass and power, +5 armor
    • Fellowship of Steel produced mechs gain 1 extra power per mass from their power source
    • Fellowship of Steel produced mech weapons gain an extra +1 damage per damage die
    • Fellowship of Steel produced mechs require that all four limbs be replaced with special, removable prosthetic limbs, costing 7,500 credits and requiring 2 system strain. These limbs can fit 2 system strain worth of Limb Cyberware and 1 system strain of Nerve Cyberware without any extra permanent system strain from cyberware.
  • Laser Weapons
    • Fellowship of Steel produced laser weapons have +2 damage (including mech, heavy, and ship laser weapons)
  • Airborne Vehicles
    • Fellowship of Steel produced airborne vehicles have +2 armor, which becomes armor equivalent to a grav tank if the armor is brought above 10 (11 or higher), +1 hardpoint, and +2 mass and power
  • Spikeships and System Ships
    • Fellowship of Steel produced ships have 50% greater maximum crew and automatically come with the vehicle transport fittings built in (no credit, mass or power cost). They also come with the following effects based on class:
      • Fighter - +1 armor, +3 hp, -2 speed
      • Frigate - +2 armor, +5 hp, -1 speed
      • Cruiser - +5 armor, +10 hp, -1 speed (min 0), cannot install Hardened Polyceramic Overlay
      • Capital - +10 armor, +25 hp, cannot install Hardened Polyceramic Overlay
    • Fellowship of Steel produced ships count as one size larger for the sake of being a detection target
  • Ground Vehicles
    • Fellowship of Steel produced ground vehicles have +2 armor, but -2 speed (to a minimum of -1 speed)
  • Robots
    • Fellowship of Steel produced robots have +1 AC, but have a -1 to hit and a -1 penalty on saves
