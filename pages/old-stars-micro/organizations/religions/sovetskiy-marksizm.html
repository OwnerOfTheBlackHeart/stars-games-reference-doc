Советский марксизм (Sovetskiy Marksizm)

  • Origin Tradition: Ideology; Buddhism (and Eastern Orthodoxy)
  • Leadership: Council
  • Evolution: Syncretism
  • Common Titles: Dorogoy Lider, Kadry (Cadre), Komitet (committee), Komissar, Professor, Monakhinya (nun)

The Sovetskiy Global’nyy Soyuz, the global state of Sovietallia, has long venerated the founder of Communism, Karl Marx.

Although the state and its people are officially atheistic, the ideas of the faiths of Buddhism and Eastern Orthodox Christianity were brought with the world's original colonists, and over time these beliefs not only blended but further became a part of the state ideology, to the point where the two were inseparable.

The people of Sovietallia believe that after death, one will reincarnate into this world again. Although new souls are created, death is never a permanent measure, and for all eternity the people will be reborn and work together in the endless struggle for equality and freedom. Despite this, however, transcending this cycle of reincarnation is possible.

Marx, to the people of Sovietallia, is a “Prosvetlennyy”, an enlightened one. He was the first to put forth the ideas of Communism, and he was in fact the latest incarnation of the holy Buddha as well as a reincarnation of Jesus Christ. As he was the latest and most important of these incarnations, the worship of these figures fell to the wayside in favor of Marx and those who followed him.

Although largely drawing from Communist ideology and Buddhism, structurally Sovietallian Marxism most resembles the Eastern Orthodox Church. There is no official head of Marxism. Instead, faith separates the world into 200 Komitet, which function as councils for the lower churches. These Komitet are each further subdivided into 16 Komissariats each, which function as regional church bodies and are each headed by a Komissar. Below the Komissar are the leaders of the churches themselves, who are called Professors.

The 200 Komitet, likewise, elect one individual to represent them in the Kadry, the council which officially leads all Komitet. The individuals elected to this office are known as “Dorogoy Lider”, Dear Leader(s).

Despite having an official body overseeing them, the various Komitet are largely independent of one another, and the primary purpose of it is largely to hand done missives from StalinNet and vote on important matters such as the canonization of new Totktoischet.

Other important leaders of Soviet history have been elevated as “Totktoischet”, one(s) who seek. These figures are venerated and prayed to for guidance just as Marx is, being viewed as somewhere between Buddhist Bodhisattvas and Christian Saints. The most important ones are:

  • Vladimir Lenin the Vechno Zhiv, ‘forever alive’ (commonly associated with knowledge, healing, and rebirth)
  • Joseph Stalin (commonly associated with strength and agriculture)
  • Nikita Khrushchev (commonly associated with science and the arts)
  • Leonid Brezhnev (commonly associated with construction, progress, and children)

In the capital of Sovietallia, Novaya Moskva, is a place known as The Red Square. The Red Square is next to the Kremlin, where the central processing node of StalinNet is, and is home to the Cathedral of the Vechno Zhiv. Appearing as a combination of a giant Russian orthodox church combined with a red pyramid, this is where the Sovietallian Mausoleum of Lenin is, which supposedly holds the still un-corrupted hand of Vladimir Lenin. This hand is regularly anointed with olive oil, which is then used in rituals by the Professors of the Cathedral. It is said to have healing properties.
