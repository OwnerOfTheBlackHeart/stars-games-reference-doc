Minor Locations

Afyood Cluster

One of the largest known clusters of Afyood, a rare herb that grows in the boulders of the Boulderlands, Afyood tap roots, which must be retrieved from within the boulder, have the ability to cure a variety of magical conditions, including some curses, faerie pox, and more.

While currently an unknown effect, the sap of an Afyood plant, when rubbed on the face of a victim within a week of exposure, can cure Skoyip's breath attack.

Aopew Ifuhn

The remains of the last city of the lobsters that once ruled Lodburg. For whatever reason, the buildings are perfectly preserved underneath layers of powdery snow. The state of the contents of the buildings, however, varies.

Athkee

An old, abandoned Rauber town. Its relative position between multiple monsters has lead to rumors that Athkee may still be unplundered and hiding some hidden riches.

Originally built by the Raubers in 762 and abandoned in 792, Athkee served to exploit Lod's coastal swamps for food and medicines while also supporting several coastal watch posts. It was destroyed by an unknown disease brought in from the swamp.

Athkee is made up of a small keep surrounded by a town, all of which has fallen into disrepair. Surrounding the town is a rotten wooden wall, of which little more than frozen timbers and reinforced mud walls remain.

Battle of Boulderborder

Rumored to be the site of a battle between a tribe of beastmen and an Om'iel scouting party, the battlefield of the Battle of Boulderborder is an oft avoided section of scared forest.

Rumors say that the bodies and gear of the slain Om'iel scouts remain where they lay, undisturbed, should they be found.

Bearman Tribe

Originally created by the fae in ages past, the beastfolk, and by extension the bearmen, tribes that have formed from the escaped beastmen are now the problems of everyone else.

This tribe of bearmen (beastfolk) normally roam far away from civilization, but have recently begun approaching Dewfrost, attacking hunters and gatherers that they come across. The reason for this is unknown, but they are a potential threat to Dewfrost.

Chillforest

Originally a sister village to Dewfrost, Chillforest was destroyed by eaglemen for an unknown reason.

Akzari was born in Chillforest. Upon entering her old home in Chillforest, Akzari will struck with a vision of her childhood, then of her teenage years, during which Chillforest comes under attack from the eaglemen, during which her and her family run. When she awakens, she will be standing before the rotten ruins of her old bed, wrapping the remains of her old blanket around her hands to create the Hand Wraps of Denial.

Coral Spires

A collection of spires made of coral frozen in clear ice and layered with snow.

A collection of coral spires that once housed the sapient lobsters that used to rule Lodburg.

The remains of the coral is preserved in layers of snow and clear ice.

Cortos Nests

A collection of nests belonging to man-sized, flying lizards known as Cortos. How they are able to withstand the cold is unknown, but most assume some kind of natural magic.

Everrain

An unending, magically created rainstorm, the Everrain pours down precipitation like a hurricane, yet nothing in the Everrain gets wet. What lay at the center of the Everrain is anyone's guess, but it is known that within the Everrain lurk giant, man-eating lobsters, tree climbing sea turtles, and schools of meat eating fish that swim through the air as if it were water.

Hunter Camp/Way Station

A collection of flimsy wooden buildings, brush huts and heavy leather tents, this location is a common way station for those hunting in the forests and swamps near the river and coast. While occasionally used or attacked by the Rauber, this camp is mainly utilized by the Axon.

The proximity to Skoyip means that the use of this camp is becoming more and more dangerous, but also more and more advantageous for guards. The hunters would like some kind of protection, probably magical, to stave off the minions of Skoyip so that they can continue to hunt and fish in peace.

Ice Wyvern

Located in the southern snow plains, the nest of a wyvern capable of spewing blizzards lies. The miles of nothing but unmelting snow have served to further protect whatever lies within the beast's territory.

The ice wyvern is actually a Shrieking Legiana created by the O'meil.

King Danrym's Keep

The keep of the giant Danrym, who has recently declared himself King of the Giants in Lodburg. Through careful diplomacy and force of arms, King Danrym has managed to extend his power over the Whispering Heights, with several other giants serving him and the rest being forced to pay "taxes."

Om'iel Portal

This portal, made by the Om'iel long again, leads to some unknown place, though neither the Axon nor the Rauber know what magics are required to activate or traverse the portal.

The runes Ul'qi and Ky'uri are required to activate the portal. Once active, the portal grants the rune Yv'kuo.

Once activated, the portal leads to an Underdark like environment, humid and warm, with almost entirely mushrooms for "plant-life." Two other destinations are available once the rune Vu'xin is known: A Mars like surface of cratered, warmish (roughly 70F), rocky desert and a series of floating islands with different environments on each.

Om'iel Pillars

These strange pillars built by the Om'iel serve no purpose that the Axon understand. Two pillars have been built, one due south of the Everrain and one hidden in the Hoarfrost Reach.

The signal pillars are actually psychic communications arrays that Om'iel sent out on scouting missions can use to communicate with the mothership.

Each pillar contains information on the language of the Om'iel, should one be able to connect to the pillar and parse the psychic barrage of information. The northern pillar contains the rune Ul'qi while the southern pillar contains the rune Ky'uri.

Order of the Golden Eagle

The Order of the Golden Eagle is a religious order based around finding the order within one's self and living a life within the laws of the Order and the land, with the Laws of Nature being the most important to the Order of the Golden Eagle, who live primarily off of hunting, fishing and gathering (during the summer).

The Order of the Golden Eagle is in conflict with the Snow Shrew Order over the location of their holy grounds.

Golden Eagle Hunting Ground

The sacred hunting grounds of the Order of the Golden Eagle. While far away from the temple where the Order of the Golden Eagle is primarily located, the Order considers it to be a sacred honor to hunt here.

The practice of hunting here has caused tension with the Snow Shrew Order, as members of the Order of the Golden Eagle disturb the Snow Shrews' sacred burial ground on their way to this hunting ground.

Oxhaven

Oxhaven was the second city founded by the Axon within Lodburg and was once prosperous with easy access to water, lumber, metals and farmland. Though nowadays, Oxhaven is a slowly decaying ruin filled with wooden statues of the people who refused to pay tribute to Skoyip, a mistake they can never make again.

Some of the lands surrounding Oxhaven have been affected with a blight that slowly kills and writhers the surrounding plant life and forces away large animals. It is said that a possible cure to the fate of Oxhaven can be found within this region, though it is also said that a cure to the blight can be found in Oxhaven.

Shallow Hill Moot

One of the primary locations for the Rauber tribes to meet for moots, where the chieftains of multiple tribes meet to discuss "federal" laws, settle inter-tribe disputes, and discuss long term plans. The Shallow Hill Moot is used to handle inter-tribe meetings, with the large, Federal Moots occurring only twice a year, once before the start of rasputitsa, on the spring equinox, and on the autumn equinox.

Skoyip's Nest

Skoyip is a pile of vines, shrubs, trees and swamp grasses that has formed together into a mystically powered plant monster vaguely resembling a dragon. As of the last few years, Skoyip has managed to begin flying and having higher thought. It is also known to be able to turn flesh to wood.

As of the last few years, Skoyip has managed to begin flying and having higher thought.

Skoyip believes itself a real dragon that is somehow incomplete, and desperately seeks to find the missing piece.

Skoyip has a breath weapon that turns flesh to wood. If the right ritual or spell is found, this effect can be reversed within a year, but after a year, no trace of who the person used to be is left.

The Snow Shrew Order

The Snow Shrew Order is a religious order focused on aligning themselves with the local spirits and fae. Like the otherworldly creatures they attempt to align with, the monks of the Snow Shrew Order, they tend to be unphased by the goings on of the world around them.

The Snow Shrew Order is in conflict with the Order of the Golden Eagle, as the Order of the Golden Eagle continues to disturb the Snow Shrews' holy burial ground.

Snow Shrew Burial Ground

The Sacred Burial Grounds of the Snow Shrew Order, which is currently a point of contention of the Snow Shrew Order and the Order of the Golden Eagle, as the Order of the Golden Eagle disturbs the burial grounds when traveling to their hunting grounds.
