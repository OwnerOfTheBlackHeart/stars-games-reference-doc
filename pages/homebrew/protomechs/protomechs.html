Protomechs

Standing 2 to 2.8 meters (6' 6" to 9' 2") and powered by high capacity batteries, or some form of fuel burning engine, protomechs are the tools of those who need better powered armor, but can't afford mechs, or who are looking to fill the tactical gap between the two capabilities. Protomechs represent the largest a powered armor system can get without the combination of micro-fusion generators and grav plates, or other fenceposting protections.

Protomechs are notably weaker than mechs, with their armor scores being similar to that of vehicles, where the damage is taken off of non-gunnery weapons while gunnery weapons completely ignore the armor. Also unlike mechs, protomechs can use a number of fittings, similar to drones. The standard battery included in a protomech lasts for 12 hours per charge, providing full life support as a suit of powered armor for that time, and takes 24 hours to charge when connected to a stable power source such as a city electric grid or starship power system.

Protomech pilots are able to use any weapons or equipment that they carry with them into battle with them. Protomechs do not have a health value of their own, requiring any pilot to take the damage themselves.

Light protomechs are barely more than powered armor and stand 10 to 20 centimeters taller than their user and can be compressed down to a 4 enc item, with every mounted item's encumbrance being added as well (gunnery weapons without a listed encumbrance count as 4). Medium protomechs stand around 2.4 meters tall and take up 2 tons of starship cargo space. Heavy protomechs stand around 2.8 meters tall and take up 5 tons of starship cargo space. Starship Vehicle Transport Fittings affect medium and heavy protomechs as if they were vehicles, with heavy protomechs rounding down to 2 tons of starship cargo space.

Modding and Building Protomechs

Protomechs use the normal item creation and modification rules, except that they are able to be built in powered armor or mech workshops, taking 1 month to assemble for a single engineer. Protomechs can accept armor mods and integrated weapons can take weapon mods.

Hulls

Hulls are the physical protomechs themselves and come in two types, military and industrial, and three weight categories, light, medium and heavy. Industrial hulls are only capable of mounting a single weapon regardless of size and provide a +2 bonus to any Strength/Exert or Constitution/Exert skill checks, and double the effect of any fittings that improve carry load. Military hulls are designed for mounting multiple weapons and have an easier time mounting armor (see the description for the Armor fitting).

Hull is the hull of the protomech. Industrial hulls are only capable of mounting a single weapon regardless of size and provide a +2 bonus to any Strength/Exert or Constitution/Exert skill checks, and double the effect of any fittings that improve carry load. Military hulls are designed for mounting multiple weapons and have an easier time mounting armor (see the description for the Armor fitting).

Cost is the cost, in credits, of a protomech on a TL 4 world with a moderate market for such equipment. TL 3 worlds multiply all protomech costs by 5 while TL 4, or higher, worlds that heavily utilize protomechs may reduce the cost by half, or even down to a quarter if protomechs are used obsessively.

AC is the base armor class provided by the protomech hull, before any fittings are built in.

Fittings is the number of fitting that the hull can handle. Note that some protomech fittings take up more than 1 fitting slot.

Fittings

Fittings, the means for protomechs to ascend beyond simple suits of armor. Some provide simple, numerical bonuses, while other provide distinct abilities and tools for the pilot of the protomech to use. Most fittings can only be applied a limited number of times, as seen in their full description. If a fitting doesn't explicitly state it can be installed more than once, it can only be installed once.

Armor fittings unsubtly layer more resilient armor plating onto the protomech, improving its ability to take a direct hit. Each instance of this fitting adds +2 armor to industrial hulls or +3 armor to military hulls. Light hulls can take this fitting once, medium hulls can take it twice and heavy hulls can take it 3 times.

A protomech with at least one instance of armor or deflective armor installed gains the same immunity to primitive weapons as powered armor.

Deflective armor fittings improve the armor shape and add protective measures, such as ablatives filled with reflective particulates, that have the chance of negating an incoming attack. Industrial hulls gain +1 AC from each instance of this fitting while military hulls gain +2 AC. Deflective armor can only be applied to a protomech twice before it no longer provides a meaningful effect.

A protomech with at least one instance of armor or deflective armor installed gains the same immunity to primitive weapons as powered armor.

Weapon mounts allow the protomech to mount a single rifle sized weapon. A cell energy weapons are powered by the protomech by default, while B cell energy weapons can be powered if a heavy weapon power system is installed in the protomech. Ammunition fed weapons, and B cell weapons, installed in weapon mounts can be reloaded normally, though installed ammo systems can reduce the need for this. Only two instances of weapon mounts can be installed on a protomech, and only heavy protomechs can mount both weapon mounts and a heavy weapon mount. Weapon mounts cannot be installed on any protomech with a mech weapon mount.

Weapon computers allow a protomech to fire both weapon mounts if two weapon mounts are installed. Due to the difficulty of managing heavy weapons on a humanoid form, weapon computers cannot be used when a weapon mounted in a heavy weapon mount is fired.

Heavy weapon mounts allow the protomech to carry a single heavy/gunnery weapon. Heavy weapon mounts can only be mounted on medium and heavy protomechs, with only protomechs able to install both heavy weapon mounts and weapon mounts. Weapons mounted in heavy weapon mounts are affected by ammo systems normally. Energy weapons mounted in a heavy weapon mount are not normally powered by the protomech, though a heavy weapon power system allows the weapon to be powered from the protomech's internal power system. Only one heavy weapon mount can be mounted onto a protomech.

Mech weapon mounts allow the protomech to carry a single suit-class mech weapon. Mech weapon mounts can only be mounted on a heavy protomech and do not allow any other type of weapon mount to be installed. Ammo mech weapons have 1 combat's worth of ammunition, with an addition combat per ammo systems allocated to the mech weapon mount. Energy mech weapons require a heavy weapon power system and a microfusion generator to operate, but require no ammunition be provided.

Ammo systems provide an extra ammunition storage and feed to a mounted weapon. For ammunition consuming and B cell weapons, each ammo system allocated to the weapon allows the carrying of another magazine of ammunition, without the need to reload. Ammunition consuming weapons and B cell weapons can be reloaded normally once all allocated ammo systems run dry. Ammo mech weapons instead gain an extra combat's worth of ammunition per allocated ammo system. There is no limit, save the number of fitting slots available, to the number of ammo systems installed on a protomech.

Heavy weapon power systems allow the protomech to internally power mounted weapons that use B cells. A heavy weapon power system and a microfusion generator is required to power mech grade energy weapons.

Microfusion generators allow a freshly refueled protomech to operate for up to 1 year before it needs refueled. Refueling a light protomech's microfusion generator requires 1 encumbrance worth of water, a medium protomech 3 encumbrance worth of water, and a heavy protomech 6 encumbrance worth of water. Only TL 4 planets can produce microfusion generators.

Bio-fuel generators allow a protomech to operate off of liquid combustibles, such as gasoline or bio-diesel. The internal tank included with the generator holds up to 12 hours of operation. Without an Oxidizer System, a bio-fuel generator cannot operate in space or in thin atmospheres. Refueling light protomechs cost 5 credits per hour of operation, medium protomechs 20 credits per hour, and heavy protomechs 50 credits per hour.

Solid bio-fuel generators allow a protomech to operate off of solid combustibles, such as wood pellets or coal. When operating off of processed fuels, such as coal or compressed wood pellets, a solid bio-fuel generator can operate for 12 hours off of its internal fuel. When operating off of unprocessed fuels, such as dry sticks, a solid bio-fuel generator can operate for 4 hours off of its internal fuel. Refueling a light protomech takes 2 encumbrance worth of fuel, a medium protomech requires 8 encumbrance worth of fuel, and a heavy protomech requires 20 encumbrance worth of fuel. Processed fuels typically cost 50 credits per encumbrance, while unprocessed fuels can typically be found by scavenging on any world with a sufficient biosphere.

Oxidizer systems allow bio-fuel generators and solid bio-fuel generators to operate in space. A single oxidizer system contains enough oxidizer to operate for 12 hours before needing refreshed.

Extended power systems increase the size of any fuel reserves or batteries used by the protomech. Each extended power system increases the maximum battery operation time by 12 hours. If an extended power system is installed in a protomech with a bio-fuel generator or a solid bio-fuel generator, it can add either a 12 hour battery or an extra maximum load of fuel for the generator, with the expected increase of cost or encumbrance to fully fuel the generator.

Gear carriers are a series of hooks, nets, pockets, and magnetic and velcro attachment points that enable the pilot of a protomech to carry more readied encumbrance. Each instance of this fitting adds +1 readied encumbrance, or +2 readied encumbrance if an industrial hull. Light hulls can install this fitting once, medium hulls twice, and heavy hulls three times.

Cargo spaces provide a protomech with the ability to carry extra weight without inhibiting the operation of the protomech, though accessing the cargo space takes time. Light protomechs carry 10 kilograms per cargo space, medium protomechs 50 kilograms per cargo space, and heavy protomechs 100 kilograms per cargo space, with industrial hulls carrying twice as much. The only limitation to how many cargo spaces a protomech can install is the available fitting slots.

Lift systems allow a protomech to lift heavy loads over-head with ease, though any load above 1 ton requires deploying the lift system's stabilization systems, which takes 1 minute to deploy or retract. The stabilization systems included in a lift system can come in many forms, though the "ant" and pulley systems are the most popular, with the "ant" system deploying six wheeled limbs that support the protomech's torso and the pulley system uses a series of pulleys that are attached to sturdy objects within 30 meters to provide support to the protomech's torso. Light protomechs can lift 100 kilograms per lift system, medium protomechs 200 kilograms per, and heavy protomechs 500 kilograms each, with industrial hulls carrying twice as much. The only limitation to how many lift systems a protomech can install is the available fitting slots. When using the lift systems, a protomech cannot fire any weapons, and moves at half speed when the stabilization systems are deployed.

Protomechs equipped with lift systems can generate exceptional forces, allowing them to tear a human sized hole in a standard postech exterior wall with four rounds of effort. Lighter or more primitive construction gives way in half the time, while fortified armorplast barriers may require 1d6 times as many rounds, or be completely impervious.

Stealth systems include low-noise servos, extra padding on the feet, thermal image reducing technologies, dull, matte paints, built-in lockpicks, and systems to break up the protomech's outline. For light and medium protomechs, stealth systems grant a +2 to stealth checks. For heavy protomech's, the stealth systems can only do so much and grant +1 to stealth checks.

Sprinting servos replace the servos and shock absorbing systems to allow the pilot to move faster than they would normally be able. Each installation of sprinting servos increases the protomech's base speed by 5 meters. Light protomechs can mount 2 sprinting servos, while gaining 10 meters of base speed per installation, medium protomechs and heavy protomechs 3.

Jump plates add a series of micro-scale grav plates and short duration capacitors that allow the protomech that allow the pilot to make sudden leaps as a move action, either horizontally or vertically. Each installation of jump plates allows the pilot to jump 10 more meters, or 15 more meters for light protomechs, as well as negate twice their jump distance in fall damage. A protomech can only mount 2 jump plates. When installed alongside void thrusters or antigrav nodes, jump plates can be used to increase flight speed per move action by the jump distance of the protomech, though doing this significantly stresses the capacitors and can only be done for up to a number of rounds equal to the number of jump plates fittings installed, requiring 1d4 rounds per round of increased movement before the jump plates can be used again.

Void thrusters installs specialized micro-scale grav plates and a continual load power system that allow the protomech to fly at its base speed in zero-gee and microgravity environments, as well to eliminate fall damage in standard gravity, even for orbital insertions.

Antigrav nodes installs specialized, high-efficiency grav plates and a continual load power system that allow the protomech to fly at its base speed in normal gravity environments, as well to eliminate fall damage in 2g gravity, even for orbital insertions. Antigrav nodes are energy intensive, and cause the protomech to expend twice as much energy or fuel while active. Protomech microfusion generators provide enough power to operate antigrav nodes without significant stress.

Advanced Sensors add integral low-light and thermal vision, basic radar sensors and high sensitivity microphones, allowing the pilot incredible awareness of their surroundings. Protomechs equipped with advanced sensors gain a +2 to all notice checks, can hear a normal conversation up to 100 meters away, can detect ground vehicles up to 1 kilometer away and aircraft up to 10 kilometers away.

Encrypted Transceivers enable the protomech to communicate with military grade encryptions equivalent to the tech level that created the fitting, as well as serving as a communications hub for nearby personnel. If no interference is present, encrypted transceivers can communicate with orbital objects, such as starships or commsats. When quantum ECM is present, encrypted transceivers can communicate with devices within 100 meters, or with other encrypted transceivers up to 1 kilometer away if a direct line of sight can be made. Other wise, encrypted transceivers can acted as an encrypted comm server or a comm set from Starvation Cheap.

Climbing Systems enable the protomech to climb any surface capable of bearing its weight at its normal movement speed. While using a climbing system, only a one handed weapon or a weapon in a weapon mounts can be used. Two handed weapons and weapons located in a heavy weapon mount or a mech weapon mount require too much of the body to be moved to be used with any effect other than boring a hole directly in front of the protomech when the climbing system is in use.

Vehicle mount systems enable the protomech to externally attach to a vehicle. While vehicles designed to carry large numbers of passengers or cargo, such as most aircraft, ATV explorers, IFVs and APCs, can fit a protomech without much issue, smaller vehicles such as civilian cars lack the internal space for the protomech effectively operate. When attached to a vehicle via vehicle mount systems, a protomech counts as 1 passenger, can only fire a single pistol sized weapon or a single weapon mount, and does not gain any protection against direct attack. Mounting a vehicle takes an Action while dismounting safely from a moving vehicle requires a Move Action. Dismounting from a stationary vehicle takes an On Turn Action. Vehicle mount systems can be installed directly into the protomech for 1 fitting slot, carried by a protomech as a 4 encumbrance item, or installed directly onto a vehicle.
