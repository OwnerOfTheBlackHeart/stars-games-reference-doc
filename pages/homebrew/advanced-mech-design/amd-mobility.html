Mobility

A mech's mobility system represents the mix of actuators, grav panels, motors, jets and artificial that make up the primary movement system of the mech, which improves its ground speed as well as its Dodge score and Speed scores of the mech. The mobility system also determines if the mech is bipedal or quadrupedal.

Bipedal mobility systems come in two distinct types: Standard and Reverse Knee. Standard mobility systems are cheap and reliable, putting the least strain on the mech's power system, but offering no special abilities. Reverse Knee mobility systems add an extra knee joint to allow the mech to store potential energy that can be released for a sudden burst of speed or to make a sudden leap without needing special fittings.

Reverse Knee mobility systems can use the potential energy stored in their reverse knees to either make a pair of long jump style leaps that allow them to move twice their normal movement in a straight line as a single movement or to leap up to half their normal movement vertically and up to half their normal movement horizontally, potentially over or onto obstacles. Reverse Knee mobility systems require 2 rounds after a leap to cool off and rebuild their potential energy before they can leap again. All ballistic weapons on a Reverse Knee equipped mech are treated as if they had the Unstable weapon quality.

Quadrupedal mobility systems come in two distinct types: Agility and Stability. Agility systems use the extra space that an extra pair of legs offer to fit in excess grav plates that grant the mech the ability to hover, while Stability systems reinforce both pairs of legs to grant a higher weight limit to the mech and make it a more stable platform.

Agility quadrupedal mobility systems allow the mech to hover up to five times its height off the ground for up to one minute. During this minute, the mech can move at in any direction, including up or down, at its normal movement speed and does not need to end its turn touching the ground. Agility quadrupedal mobility systems require a number of rounds equal to twice the time spent hovering to cool down before hovering again.

Stability quadrupedal mobility systems aid in aiming, especially while stationary, ignoring the Unstable weapon quality and, thanks to handling recoil better than bipedal legs, grant a +1 modifier to hit with ballistic weapons when remaining stationary in the turn that the weapon is fired, as per the Accurate weapon special quality's Aim action.
