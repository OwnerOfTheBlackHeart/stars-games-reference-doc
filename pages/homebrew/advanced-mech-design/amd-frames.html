Frames

A frame consists of two parts: The frame itself and the power source. The frame determines the health points, dodge, base speed and maximum mass of the mech, while the power source takes up mass to provide maximum power to the mech.

A frame comes in one of three sizes (Light, Medium, Heavy), one of four forms (Static, Performance, Balanced and Rugged) and one of three qualities (Subpar, Average, and Exceptional), and can be quadrupedal. If a frame is not quadrupedal, then it is bipedal. A frame can also be Static. Static frames are purpose built around their engine, parts and fittings, with only the weapons being reasonably simple to swap, and any major change beyond basic repairs and maintenance (or a weapon swap) requires a week of dedicated reconfiguring by a trained technician in a fully equipped mech modification bay. However, Static frames are cheaper to build and maintain, easier to mass produce, able to fit into a smaller footprint, and generally built for second line work.

Light frames are typically 4 to 6 meters tall, medium frames 6 to 8 meters tall and heavy frames 8 to 10 meters tall. Static frames, thanks to being purpose built around their components, are typically about 3 / 4 the height of a normal frame.

Power sources come in three forms: Battery, Engine and Fusion. Battery power sources are large battery packs, which make for the cheapest power sources and see frequent use on Static frames, and last 24 hours on a single charge with 4 hours of operation recharged per hour of charging. Engine power sources are some form of fuel engine, such as a hydrogen fuel cell or internal combustion engine, whose improved Mass efficiency and acceptable cost make them a common choice for mechs. A single fueling lasts for 24 hours of operation and requires 30 minutes to fully refill the fuel tank, with light mechs costing 100 credits per fueling, medium mechs 250 credits per fueling and heavy mechs 1,000 credits per fueling. Fusion power sources are micro-fusion generators placed within the mech, which can last upwards of a decade on a single fuel charge, have outstanding Power to Mass ratios and a price tag that makes them a rare choice for mech power source.

A mech that does not require extra power is assumed to have a Battery power source.
